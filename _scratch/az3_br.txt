Process Notes
All activities are performed at 15 C to 27C.
For all calculations, assume 1 L = 1 kg and 1 mL = 1 g unless otherwise noted. PK-4500 absorbance units are in mAU. The conversion from OD to mAU is: mAU = OD x 0.15 x 1000.
The target linear velocity for CaptureSelect CH1-XL Chromatography is 200 cm/hr except for the ELUTION step where the linear velocity is 150 cm/hr. Alert limits are +/- 5% of target and the Normal Operating Range is +10% of the target.
Unless otherwise noted, flow rate ranges are for steady state operation and do not consider pump ramping strategy.
NOTE: Temporary flowrate excursions resulting from the skid adjusting to changing process conditions are acceptable.
Verify that all solutions, raw materials, and standards are within expiration prior to use.
When an equipment ID is written in the MPR, the operator is indicating they have visually inspected and verified calibration prior to use.
During this process if PAUSE is pushed on the chromatography skid HMI the chromatography skid pumps will stop. If PAUSE is used at any time during this process the details as to why PAUSE was pushed must be documented in Attachment 6: Comments Section. To continue processing, push the CONTINUE button.
During the load step, next breakpoint may be used to proceed to the next chromatography step in the recipe. This should be used when the operator is sure that the load step is complete. Note the use of this in the MPR at the step in which it is used.
If the next breakpoint is used for any other reason a supervisor, or designee, signature is required. Record the reason in Attachment 6: Comments Section.
NOTE:  This function does not apply to the elution step and should not be used during elution unless directed by the area supervisor to do so.
The chromatography process, outlined in this MPR, may be run manually if the automated chromatography recipe is not functioning correctly. (Move to the recipe section)
If a non-routine sample is requested by MEMO, complete Attachment 2: Non-Routine Sampling Record. Complete record by recording the Process Section No., Contents, Container Type, Quantity, Designation, Perform by/Date, and Witness by/Date fields.
NOTE: If Non-Routine samples are taken, re-adjust the weight of the product sampled.
All parameters listed in this document are Targets/Alert Limits unless otherwise noted. When alert limits are exceeded, contact Supervisor and Downstream Tech Transfer. Document any actions taken in Attachment 6: Comments Section.
To avoid over pressurization during process, flow rates may be lowered manually or as part of the method. Record adjusted flowrate information in Attachment 4: Adjusted Flowrate Table.
